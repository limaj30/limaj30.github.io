Why I Love Video Games

09-25-2025 | Random Thoughts

The earliest memory I have of playing video games is on my brother’s original Xbox. I can’t even recall the game, but I do remember hanging out with him that day—and that was my first introduction to the gaming world.

My more vivid memories came later, when Call of Duty became my second home (I know, sad). But honestly, where I grew up there were a lot of gangs—and there still are—so in a weird way, video games kept me safe. They gave me a reason to stay inside, hop on with my friends, and avoid a lot of the stuff that was happening outside.

Don’t get me wrong though, I wasn’t just a full-time homebody. I still joined after-school programs, played varsity soccer and volleyball in high school, and even ran with the running club back in middle school. But when the day wound down, you could bet I was in a CoD lobby or a FIFA (now EA FC) LAN tournament.

Nothing beat the chaos of screaming at each other in game lobbies before a match (if you know, you know). The trash talk was ruthless, and the celebrations after a last-minute FIFA goal were pure comedy. It was loud, chaotic, and honestly, some of the best memories I have with friends.

For me, video games became a way to decompress and socialize—without the pressure of “real world” expectations. It was hanging out, but from the comfort of home (or surrounded by snacks and broken controllers).

Looking back, gaming has been there through every stage of my life. From middle school CoD lobbies to late-night FIFA rivalries, it’s more than just a hobby—it’s a shared experience, a way to bond, and one of my favorite escapes.

At the end of the day, that’s why I love video games. They’re fun, frustrating, sometimes ridiculous—but they’ve given me memories, friendships, and moments I wouldn’t trade for anything.

So I’ll end this with a question: What’s your earliest gaming memory?
